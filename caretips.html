Plant Care Tips

Expert advice to help your plants thrive

Watering

Most plants need consistent moisture, but overwatering is one of the most common mistakes. Check the soil before watering—it should be dry to the touch about 1-2 inches below the surface. Water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom, then empty the saucer to prevent root rot.

Sunlight

Understanding your plant's light requirements is crucial. Most indoor plants prefer indirect bright light. Place them near windows but avoid direct afternoon sun which can scorch leaves. South-facing windows provide the most light, while north-facing provide the least.

Temperature & Humidity

Most houseplants thrive in temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C). Avoid placing plants near heating vents, air conditioners, or drafty windows. Many tropical plants appreciate humidity—mist leaves regularly or use a pebble tray with water.

Soil & Fertilizer

Use well-draining potting mix appropriate for your plant type. Fertilize during the growing season(spring and summer) with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer diluted to half strength. Reduce fertilizing in fall and winter when plant growth slows.

Pruning & Maintenance

Regular pruning encourages bushier growth and removes dead or yellowing leaves. Use clean, sharp scissors or pruning shears. Wipe leaves periodically to remove dust and allow better photosynthesis. Rotate plants weekly for even growth.

Pest Control

Inspect plants regularly for pests like aphids, spider mites, or mealybugs. Isolate affected plants immediately. Treat with insecticidal soap or neem oil. Prevention is key—keep plants healthy with proper care and quarantine new plants for 2 weeks.

Repotting

Repot plants every 1-2 years or when roots grow through drainage holes. Choose a pot 1-2 inches larger in diameter. Spring is the best time to repot. Use fresh potting mix and water thoroughly after repotting.

General Care

Every plant is unique—research specific care requirements for your species. Observe your plants daily and learn their signals. Drooping leaves might need water, while yellow leaves could indicate overwatering. Patience and consistency are key to plant care success!
