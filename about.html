ABOUT US

We are a group of students who created this website as part of our field project to promote awareness about local plant life. Our goal is to make it easy for visitors to learn more about the trees and plants around them through technology. By scanning the QR codes placed in Teli Gali Garden & Park, users can instantly access detailed information, photos, and interesting facts about each plant. This project combines our interest in nature and web development to encourage more people to appreciate and understand the greenery in their surroundings.
Project Summary

Our project has developed a user-friendly webpage designed to guide users through detailed information about various plant species. The webpage provides essential details in the form of cards that includes plant's image, common name, scientific name, type, height, life span, oxygen level, and a comprehensive description. Additionally, users can access further information through a Google link and explore video perspectives via a YouTube link.

Plants Database

Our plant database includes a total of 37 flora within Teli Gali, with 37 unique plant species identified. The data, which is 100% primary, has been carefully collected through field research and digitally documented. All plant information — including names, types, and key attributes is stored as text files in the Gist directory on GitHub for transparency and future reference.

Team Management

Our project is developed by a team of four dedicated students — Sahil, Carol, Tanvi, and Kavya — under the guidance of our mentor, Ms. Jyoti Chauhan. Each team member contributed their skills and ideas to ensure the project was completed successfully. With our mentor’s constant support and direction, we were able to combine field research with technology to create an interactive website that helps people learn more about the plants in Teli Gali Garden & Park. Through teamwork, creativity, and commitment, we aimed to make environmental learning simple, digital, and engaging for everyone.

Target

Our main target is to make plant identification and learning simple, digital, and accessible for everyone. The website helps visitors easily explore information about each plant within the college and nearby gardens. By scanning the QR codes placed on trees, users can view plant details, images, and interesting facts instantly. Through this initiative, we aim to promote environmental awareness, encourage curiosity about nature, and inspire more people to appreciate the green spaces around them.
